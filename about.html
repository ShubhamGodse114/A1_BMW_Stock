History

The Otto Flugmaschinenfabrik was founded in 1910 by Gustav Otto. It was reorganized into Bayerische Flugzeugwerke AG in 1916, and later became BMW in 1922.

BMW began as an aircraft engine manufacturer, producing the BMW IIIa in 1917. After World War I, it shifted to motorcycle and equipment production. In 1923, BMW released its first motorcycle, the R 32.

BMW entered the automobile market in 1928 by acquiring Fahrzeugfabrik Eisenach. Its first car, the BMW 3/15, was based on the Austin 7. The brand gained popularity in the 1930s for luxury and sports cars.

During World War II, BMW focused on aircraft engines and used forced labor. Post-war, the company shifted to household items and bicycles before resuming motorcycle and car production in the 1950s.
